What Is Private Market Investing?

Private market investing is the allocation of capital to assets and businesses not listed on public exchanges, including agricultural operations, agribusiness portfolios, and private equity, typically accessed through managed platforms, direct agreements, or syndicate structures.

Private market investing refers to investing in assets not listed or traded on public exchanges, including agricultural assets, agribusiness operations, private equity, and private credit. Unlike public markets, private market investments are typically held for defined periods and offer higher return potential in exchange for lower liquidity. In emerging markets like Africa, private market assets, particularly in agriculture and agribusiness, have historically delivered strong real returns tied to tangible economic activity.

Why Emerging Markets?

Emerging markets offer the growth differential, demographic dividend, and productivity gap that make private market investment particularly compelling. Africa alone is projected to have the world's largest working-age population by 2035, creating sustained demand growth for food, services, and infrastructure that drives the underlying assets in which we invest.

Common Questions
What is private investing? +

Private investing is the practice of allocating capital to assets and businesses not listed on public exchanges, including agricultural operations, private equity, and private credit, typically through direct agreements, managed funds, or structured investment platforms.

How is private market investing different from buying stocks? +

Public markets offer liquidity and daily pricing but are subject to volatility and sentiment-driven price movements. Private markets offer less liquidity but typically higher returns, lower volatility relative to underlying asset performance, and real-asset backing in sectors like agriculture.
